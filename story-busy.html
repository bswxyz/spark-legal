How to keep the spark alive on a busy week

When life is full, connection doesn't need a reservation or a free evening. It needs small, repeatable moments. Here are seven that punch above their weight.

1. The 10‑minute check‑in

Pick a recurring slot — coffee, the drive, lights‑out — and ask one real question instead of logistics. "What was the best part of your day, and the hardest?" beats "did you call the plumber?"

2. Trade one phone for one question

Before the scroll, ask each other something you don't already know. Curiosity is the antidote to feeling like roommates.

3. The 6‑second kiss

Relationship researchers love this one: a kiss long enough to actually feel. It resets the nervous system and says "us" in a way a peck doesn't.

4. Name the small thing

Once a day, tell them one specific thing you appreciated — not "you're great," but "thank you for handling dinner when I was fried." Specific gratitude lands.

5. A standing 15‑minute date

Same night each week, no errands allowed. Even on the couch. Protecting the slot matters more than the activity.

6. Keep a tiny record

Snap a photo, save a one‑line note. Looking back at your own week together is a surprisingly strong glue.

7. Go a little deeper on purpose

Once a week, ask one question that isn't safe — about a dream, a worry, a want. Depth is a muscle; busy weeks are exactly when it atrophies.
